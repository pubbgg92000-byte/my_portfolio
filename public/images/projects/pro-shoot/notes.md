## Business-facing case-study notes

* **[Required] Business context explored:**
  Pro Shoot was built as a learning project around the structure of a photography or creative-service website—one that needs to do more than just look good. The project explores how a visually driven business website can present work samples, communicate services, build trust, and guide visitors toward an inquiry or booking action through a clear, polished digital experience.

* **[Required] Target audience:**
  The intended audience is people evaluating a creative service or photography business—such as individuals, brands, or businesses looking for portfolio proof, service clarity, and an easy way to understand the offering before making contact. The site is designed to help a visitor move from “this looks good” to “I understand the work and know how to inquire.”

* **[Required] Solution direction:**
  The project balances **brand impression**, **work showcase**, **service explanation**, and **contact/inquiry flow** within a premium visual layout. Rather than behaving like a generic image gallery, the site is structured to guide the visitor through:

  * first impression / visual identity
  * proof of work through curated imagery
  * clarity around services or categories
  * trust-building content such as process, story, or positioning
  * a clear path toward inquiry or next action

* **[Required] Page / section structure:**
  Confirm against the actual build, but the case-study direction can be framed around sections such as:

  * hero / homepage introduction
  * featured portfolio or gallery section
  * service categories or photography offerings
  * about / brand or creative-story section
  * process, package, or “how I work” section
  * testimonials / trust-building content *(only if it actually exists and is truthful)*
  * contact or inquiry CTA
  * responsive mobile treatment that preserves the premium presentation without crowding the content

* **[Required] Visual and brand presentation:**
  The visual direction should be documented against the real build, but the current framing can include:

  * premium, image-led layout with a strong first-screen impression
  * spacious sections and clean hierarchy to let photography or featured visuals breathe
  * typography and section rhythm that support a more editorial / luxury / service-business feel
  * clear calls to action without making the site feel overly sales-heavy
  * a visual balance between inspiration, trust, and practical information

* **[Required] Customer journey / service storytelling:**
  The intended user journey is something like:

  * arrive through the homepage / hero and understand the creative tone of the brand
  * browse selected work or category examples
  * understand what services or kinds of shoots / projects are offered
  * build trust through story, presentation, and supporting sections
  * reach a clear contact or inquiry action without needing to hunt for next steps

  The site should feel like a business-facing presentation system, not just a loose portfolio gallery.

  * responsive image handling / gallery layout techniques

  * performance decisions around large imagery and media-heavy layouts
  * form handling or inquiry flow if a contact section is part of the build

* **[Required] Challenges and decisions:**

  * balancing visual richness with clarity so the site feels premium rather than cluttered
  * deciding how much of the site should function like a portfolio versus a business website
  * structuring galleries and service sections so they support conversion instead of distracting from it
  * making a photography / creative-business site feel distinctive without relying on excessive motion or visual noise
  * handling large imagery, responsive layout, and pacing so the experience works well across screen sizes
  * using AI assistance to speed up planning and iteration while still keeping authorship and decision-making clear

* **[Required] Learnings:**
  Pro Shoot helped strengthen my understanding of:

  * how creative-service websites differ from internal tools or product dashboards
  * structuring a website around **brand, proof, service clarity, and inquiry flow**
  * designing responsive galleries and image-led layouts without losing usability
  * using hierarchy, rhythm, and calls to action to support conversion-aware UX
  * balancing premium visual presentation with performance, accessibility, and maintainable frontend structure
  * using AI tools as a support layer for ideation, implementation help, and refinement while still making the key frontend and UX decisions myself
